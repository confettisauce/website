May 19th

Sintra exploration day!

My recommendation is to get an early start on this day, as it can become jammed with tourists the later the day goes on. I also recommend packing snacks, SPF, and water.

Getting to Sintra via public transportation could not be any easier! After fueling up with a good breakfast, walk 12 minutes from your hotel to the Cais do Sodré train station. Trains to Sintra leave twice every hour.

After arriving in Sintra via train, you'll want to purchase bus tickets for the 434. This is the tourist hop on/hop off sightseeing bus. The bus ticket office is opposite the train station, you can't miss it upon arrival. Purchase the 434 loop ticket.

After hopping on the bus, ride it until it stops at Castelo Dos Mouros, a 10th century castle. Tickets can be purchased upon arrival.

Hop back on the 434 and ride to The National Palace of Pena, a rainbow painted palace surrounded by explorable park greenery and ruins. Tickets can be purchased upon arrival.

Hop back on the 434 and ride it to the historic town center, where you can relax for a bit and get something to eat. Notable options include:

  • Casa das Minas
  • Cantinho da Vila
  • Romaria de Baco
  • Tacho Real
  • Pizzeria Do Carmo

After lunch, walk 10-15 minutes to Quinta da Regaleira, a palace estate with gardens, a chapel, and an on site museum.

When you're finished exploring, walk back to the town center bus stop and ride the 434 back to the train station to head back to your hotel.

8:30pm: Dinner reservations at the gorgeous Javá Rooftop.

This restaurant has your credit card on file because it is a €10 per person charge if you no-show. They hold the table for 15 minutes. I also discovered the outdoor area is apparently pet friendly so fingers crossed you see some dogs!
